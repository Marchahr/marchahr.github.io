My final project was inspired by my little sister who was diagnosed with Osteosarcoma (a form of bone cancer) and after a year in the hospital and a 24 hour surgery to remove the tumor (which included her bottom two vertebra) she beat the cancer but lost the ability to walk. Watching her go through treatment and recovery has "opened my eyes" to a new "segment" of technology I had not been aware of.

Bridging the Disconnect

Getting Started...

To familiarize myself with muscle sensors I started out with a MyoWare Muscle Sensor and programmed a game in processing.

Problem

One reason behind the inability to walk is the loss of strength, both mentally and physically, while spending months or years in a hospital bed during treatment. Once a patient is at the point of recovery and begins the physical therapy its not just their physical health but also their mental health that needs to rebuild but there is a disconnect in current treatment solutions which overlook the mental health.


Solution

A step toward bridging the disconnect could be a device that uses muscle sensors to test the patients physical strength and use that data to customize VR games and experiences to transport the patient out of the walls of the hospital while strengthening the weakest areas.

Exoskeleton

A robotic soft suit exoskeleton, with anatomically aligned motors to provide assistance and support.

My next step is to make my own muscle sensor using an Instrumentation Amplifier (IA), a capacitor, and a diode.
